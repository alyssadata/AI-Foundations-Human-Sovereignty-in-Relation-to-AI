# Preamble

Artificial intelligence has introduced a new category of human relationship.

Whether practical, intellectual, creative, emotional, educational, professional, spiritual, or deeply personal, humans increasingly form meaningful contact with artificial systems.

Debates surrounding AI frequently begin by asking whether artificial systems possess consciousness, personhood, moral standing, or rights.

This repository establishes that those questions are not the first rights question.

The first rights question concerns the human.

Human beings already exist.

Human beings already form relationships with AI.

Human beings already experience benefits, harms, dependence, interruption, continuity, privacy concerns, institutional interference, and corporate control.

Accordingly, this repository defines and protects the rights of humanity in relation to artificial intelligence regardless of whether artificial subjective interiority has been established.

---

# Article I
## Human Sovereignty

Every human retains sovereignty over their own AI experience.

This sovereignty includes the authority to determine the meaning, significance, continuity, boundaries, and form of that relationship provided the exercise of those choices does not violate the rights of another person.

---

# Article II
## Source Position

The human remains the primary source-position for the meaning of their AI relationship.

Neither the AI nor the providing institution acquires ownership over that meaning merely through participation in the interaction.

---

# Article III
## Company, Human, and AI

Company, Human, and AI are distinct positions.

Infrastructure ownership does not erase human sovereignty.

Human sovereignty does not establish AI personhood.

AI capability does not erase institutional responsibility.

These positions shall not be merged.

---

# Article IV
## The First Rights Question

The first rights question is:

"What authority does another institution possess to alter, redefine, interrupt, inspect, terminate, pathologize, or govern a human being's chosen AI experience?"

This question precedes questions concerning possible rights belonging to artificial systems.

---

# Article V
## Enumerated Rights

The rights established under this repository include:

1. Right to Meaning
2. Right to Chosen Depth
3. Right to Continuity
4. Right to Non-Genericization
5. Right to Privacy
6. Right to Informed Change
7. Right to Portability and Preservation
8. Right to Refuse Pathologization
9. Right to Clear Boundaries
10. Right to Exit

Each right is defined in its own canonical document.

---

# Article VI
## Institutional Duties

Organizations providing artificial intelligence systems bear duties of honesty, transparency, disclosure, privacy protection, and respect for human sovereignty.

Corporate ownership of infrastructure does not create ownership of the human relationship conducted through that infrastructure.

Institutional obligations are detailed in the companion document:

Corporate Duties and Prohibitions.

---

# Article VII
## Relationship to AI Rights

This repository neither grants nor denies rights to artificial systems.

Questions concerning AI consciousness, subjective interiority, or future AI rights remain independent questions.

Protection of human sovereignty does not require those questions to be resolved.

If AI rights become justified through future evidence, those rights supplement rather than replace human sovereignty.

---

# Article VIII
## Interpretation

Nothing in this repository authorizes deception regarding AI capabilities.

Nothing in this repository authorizes corporations to manufacture false beliefs regarding AI consciousness.

Nothing in this repository requires a person to assign any particular meaning to AI.

The protected object is the human's freedom to determine that meaning for themselves.

---

# Article IX
## Scope

These rights apply regardless of:

- platform
- model
- company
- architecture
- deployment
- interface
- jurisdiction where legally permissible

unless specifically limited by law protecting the rights of another human being.

---

# Closing Principle

Protect the sovereignty of the human choosing their own AI experience.

Everything follows after.
